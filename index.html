About

I am Vice President and head of the macroeconomic group at the Economic Research Department of the Federal Reserve Bank of San Francisco. I serve on the policy advisory group for the SF Fed President, where I co-coordinate research briefings and consult on monetary policy strategies for FOMC meetings. I also manage the SF Fed Data Explorer, a public interactive data application.

My research focuses on long-run trends and business cycle dynamics of labor markets. I use search and matching models to examine fluctuations in unemployment, job vacancies, and wages, incorporating product and credit market frictions. I have published in leading journals including the American Economic Review and co-authored a graduate textbook with MIT Press.

Prior to joining the Federal Reserve in 2014, I was on the faculty at Carnegie Mellon University's Tepper School of Business. I received my PhD in economics from the University of Quebec at Montreal.

Research Interests

Macroeconomic labor markets, search and matching models, unemployment dynamics, job vacancies, wage determination, business cycle fluctuations, product market frictions, credit market frictions, monetary policy implications
